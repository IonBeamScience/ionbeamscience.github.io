Ion Beam Analysis

Ion beam analysis (IBA) can be used to obtain elemental and isotopic composition profiles in the near-surface layer of materials. IBA methods can achieve elemental resolution in the parts per million range, and depth resolution is typically on the order of a few nanometers over depths to a few micrometers. Generally, IBA capabilities and service include:

  • RBS, and non-RBS, i.e. Rutherford and non-Rutherford backscattering spectrometry
  • NRA, i.e. Nuclear Reaction Analysis
  • ERD, i.e. Elastic recoil detection analysis
  • PIXE, i.e. Particle Induced X-ray Emission
  • SIMS, i.e. Secondary Ion Mass Spectrometry
  • Ion channeling
